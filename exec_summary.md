# FindIt — Campus Lost & Found Portal
### Executive Summary for the Dean's Office

---

**The Problem**

Every semester, hundreds of items are lost across campus — wallets, ID cards, laptops, keys, water bottles. The current system is a physical notice board in the admin block: handwritten notes that fade, get torn down, and reach maybe 5% of the student body. Students have resorted to WhatsApp groups and Instagram stories, but these are chaotic, unsearchable, and items scroll out of view within hours. The result: most lost items are never reunited with their owners, even when a well-meaning finder picks them up.

**The Solution**

FindIt is a digital lost-and-found portal built specifically for our campus. It takes under 10 seconds to post a lost or found item (name, email, photo, description — no account creation needed), and our smart matching engine immediately surfaces probable matches across all listings. When a likely match is found, both parties are notified instantly. A structured claim-and-confirm workflow ensures items are returned to the right person, not just the first person to ask.

**How It Works**

A student loses their wallet in the cafeteria. They open FindIt, tap "I Lost Something," fill in a quick form with a photo and description, and hit Post. Meanwhile, another student found a wallet in the same area an hour earlier and posted it as a Found item. FindIt's matching engine — which scores items across category, description, location, timing, and even image similarity — flags this as a 78% match and notifies both students. The finder reviews the owner's claim (which includes identifying details only the real owner would know), confirms it, and they arrange a handover. Total time from lost to found: under 2 hours instead of never.

**Key Design Principles**

The Dean's directive was clear: *"It should feel as easy to use as Instagram, not as boring as a college portal."* FindIt was built with that philosophy at its core. The interface is card-based and visual, not form-heavy and bureaucratic. Photos are front and center. Interactions are one-tap where possible. There are no passwords to remember, no admin approvals to wait for, no forms to print. It is mobile-friendly by design — because students will open this on their phones, not a desktop.

**Impact Potential**

Based on peer institutions that have deployed similar digital systems, we can expect:
- 60-70% of lost items to be posted digitally (vs. <10% on the physical board)
- A 3-5x improvement in item return rates
- Significant reduction in admin staff time spent fielding lost-item inquiries
- Improved student satisfaction and a tangible signal that the institution invests in student experience

**What's Next**

FindIt is live and ready for a pilot with one department or hostel block. A campus-wide rollout requires only a link on the student portal and a brief announcement. Future enhancements — campus SSO integration, a mobile app, and an admin analytics dashboard — can be built incrementally based on usage data from the pilot.
